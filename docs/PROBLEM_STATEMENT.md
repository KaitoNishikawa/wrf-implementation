We study the problem of closed-loop wildfire monitoring and short-horizon spread prediction using a single UAV with limited sensing range and flight time. The wildfire is represented as a latent spatiotemporal field whose evolution is governed by partially known fire-spread physics and uncertain environmental factors such as wind, terrain, and fuel distribution. The UAV must sequentially select dynamically feasible sensing locations to update a physics-informed Gaussian process posterior over the evolving fire field. Unlike conventional informative path planning methods that prioritize reconstruction of a static spatial map, our objective is prediction-aware: the UAV should collect measurements that are most informative for both the current fire-front estimate and its future evolution. This leads to a coupled active perception and planning problem in which UAV motion determines future observations, observations reshape the physics-informed posterior, and the posterior determines both fire-spread prediction and subsequent sensing actions.

Accordingly, three potential contributions we could target are:
1. A physics-informed spatiotemporal GP model for evolving wildfire fields: We will develop a Gaussian process model that encodes wildfire spread structure, such as wind-driven advection, anisotropic diffusion, terrain/fuel-dependent spread rates, or front-propagation dynamics. The goal is to infer not only the current fire-front or intensity field from sparse UAV observations, but also a short-horizon predictive distribution over future wildfire evolution.
2. A prediction-aware active sensing objective for single-UAV monitoring: We will formulate an informative planning objective that selects UAV sensing actions based on their expected value for future fire-spread prediction, rather than only reducing current map uncertainty. This allows the UAV to prioritize observations near dynamically important regions, such as active fire fronts, high-wind corridors, high-fuel areas, or regions where uncertainty strongly affects downstream spread prediction.
3. A closed-loop UAV planning framework under sensing, dynamics, and safety constraints: We will design a receding-horizon planner that couples the physics-informed GP posterior with UAV motion planning. The planner will account for limited field of view, flight-time or battery constraints, vehicle dynamics, altitude limits, and minimum standoff distance from hazardous fire regions, enabling safe and adaptive wildfire monitoring with a single drone.
