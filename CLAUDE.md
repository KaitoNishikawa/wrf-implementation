# CLAUDE.md

## Project Overview

This project explores physics-informed Gaussian Processes for wildfire spread prediction.

Ground truth is generated by `simulation.ipynb` (repo root, read-only), which implements:

- Level-set wildfire propagation
- Rothermel rate-of-spread equations
- WRF fire-spread methodology

Do not modify `simulation.ipynb`.

## Primary References

Read paper notes from `gp/docs/` only. Contains:

1. Numerical Gaussian Processes for Time-dependent and Non-linear PDEs
2. An Accurate Fire-Spread Algorithm in the WRF Model Using the Level-Set Method
3. A Mathematical Model for Predicting Fire (Rothermel Model)

## Modeling Philosophy

Keep implementations structurally simple: minimize unnecessary abstractions and failure points.

Do not sacrifice fidelity to the Rothermel level-set equations and constants in `simulation.ipynb`.

Prefer a clear, auditable physics encoding over an opaque shortcut.

Use Euler discretization only.

Avoid:

- Runge-Kutta formulations
- Complex boundary treatments (assume fire spread never reaches the boundary, so boundary conditions are not necessary)
- Frameworks or techniques that obscure the underlying physics

## Approach

You have freedom to choose how to embed Rothermel physics into the Numerical GP framework.

Each trial may use a different encoding strategy. Before presenting results, write a detailed trial report at `gp/results/trial_NNN/report.tex` following the **Trial Documentation** section below. Start from [`gp/report_template.tex`](gp/report_template.tex).

Fidelity to `simulation.ipynb` matters more than following a fixed recipe.

## Numerical GP requirements (non-negotiable)

These are hard constraints. A trial that violates any of them is invalid regardless of how good the metrics look. The failure mode they guard against: a parametric mean reconstructs the exact known initial condition, the GP residual collapses to zero, and the "prediction" is just the ground-truth simulator re-run from that field — which yields excellent-looking metrics while the GP does no real work.

**The GP must produce the predictions.** Predictions at t = 50…300 must come from the numerical-GP time-stepper — the Euler discretization embedded in the GP, with covariance propagated through the (linearized) operator as in the Numerical GP paper. Predictions must **not** be produced by calling the ground-truth simulator's forward step. Ground truth and prediction must be separate code paths that share no forward-stepping function.

**Propagate uncertainty, not just the mean.** A pure mean-only rollout — advancing only the posterior mean and discarding covariance after t = 0 — is not a valid trial on its own. The GP must carry a covariance/variance to every evaluation time. Reporting the mean is fine, but a non-trivial predictive variance at t > 0 is required.

**The GP must be load-bearing.** If the GP residual, posterior variance, or contribution collapses to ~zero, that is a failure signal, not a result. The report must include a diagnostic that proves the GP does non-trivial work (e.g. residual magnitude, predictive variance at t > 0, or an ablation showing the result changes when the GP is removed).

**No leakage of the ground truth.** The model sees only the 50 sampled observation **values**. It must never use:

- the full t = 0 field, its analytic functional form, or its generating parameters (e.g. "the ignition is a square signed-distance field centered at (100, 100) with half-side 8");
- any ground-truth geometry hard-coded as a default, search seed, or initializer.

Read `simulation.ipynb` for **physics, constants, and operators only**. Do not use its initial-condition construction as a reconstruction prior — that makes the sparse-observation inference trivial and defeats the experiment. The initial field must be genuinely inferred from the 50 observations under a prior general enough that the inference is non-trivial.

## Trial Documentation

Every trial must include a detailed write-up at `gp/results/trial_NNN/report.tex`. Compile to `gp/results/trial_NNN/report.pdf` with `pdflatex report.tex`. This is for your own review — not a publishable paper. Skip abstract, introduction, and background context you already know.

Write it in the **mathematical style** of *Numerical Gaussian Processes for Time-dependent and Non-linear Partial Differential Equations* (`gp/docs/`): numbered equations, operator notation, block matrix / multi-output GP structure, and tables — not prose-heavy exposition.

### Style requirements

- Use LaTeX as the source format; copy and adapt `gp/report_template.tex` for each trial.
- Lead with equations and definitions; keep prose short and functional.
- Number equations with `\begin{equation}...\end{equation}` and `\label{eq:...}`; reference with `\eqref{eq:...}`.
- Define every symbol when first introduced.
- Show the key mathematical objects explicitly, including where applicable:
  - the level-set / PDE operator (e.g. `\mathcal{L}_x`)
  - the Euler update
  - GP priors (e.g. `\phi^{n-1}(\mathbf{x}) \sim \mathcal{GP}(\ldots)`)
  - joint multi-output GP structure as block matrices using `\begin{bmatrix}...\end{bmatrix}`
  - kernel expressions derived from the time-stepper
  - conditioning / inference equations for sparse observations
- Use `booktabs` tables for constants (from `simulation.ipynb`), hyperparameters, and metrics.
- Include figures with `\includegraphics` pointing to PNGs in the same trial directory.
- Cite sources in the Sources section: Numerical GP paper, Rothermel/WRF papers, `simulation.ipynb`.
- Flag approximations and assumptions explicitly (e.g. operator linearization, kernel approximations, ignored boundaries, nonlinear term treatment). Mean-only propagation is no longer an acceptable approximation on its own — see **Numerical GP requirements**.
- Keep **Method** (what was built), **Results** (what happened), and **Assumptions** (what was simplified) as separate sections.

### Required structure for `report.tex`

Use these `\section{}` headings (see `gp/report_template.tex`):

- **Governing Physics** — level-set evolution and Rothermel RHS (numbered equations), constants table
- **Method** — subsections: Prior placement; Euler discretization and numerical GP construction; Inference from sparse observations; Algorithm
- **Assumptions and approximations** — itemized list of every simplification
- **Experimental setup** — seed, grid size, $\Delta t$, $\Delta x$, evaluation times, code paths
- **Results** — metrics table, `\includegraphics` references to trial figures
- **Sources** — citations to papers and `simulation.ipynb`
- **Notes (optional)** — brief bullets on what worked, what failed, next-trial ideas

## Physics

The physics embedded in the GP should be derived from the Rothermel Model paper. Use the Rothermel-based level-set evolution equation in `simulation.ipynb` as reference.

The simulator implementation is the source of truth for:

- constants
- parameter values
- equations

## Evaluation Protocol

Use a fixed random seed (document the value used).

Sample 50 observations from t = 0 for all experiments. Draw them from a **fixed central 100$\times$100 window** of the grid (indices $50 \le x, y < 150$), not the full domain. This window is ground-truth-blind — it is the center of the domain, not chosen from the (unknown) front location; it concentrates the sparse sample where the dynamics live so the front is inferable under a general prior, while keeping the inference genuinely sparse (50 of 10{,}000 cells).

Generate comparisons at t = 50, 100, 150, 200, 250, 300.

For each evaluation:

- show prediction and ground truth side-by-side
- at t = 0, show the initial level-set field with sampled observation points overlaid
- report comparison metrics (at minimum field RMSE; fire-front contour distance if feasible)
- save all outputs under `gp/results/trial_NNN/`

### Evaluation integrity

The evaluation must be a real test — the prediction must be able to fail. Enforce:

- **Separate code paths.** Ground truth comes from the simulator; predictions come from the GP. They must not share a forward-stepping function, or the comparison is circular and cannot detect error.
- **Symmetric reinitialization.** Any redistancing/reinitialization of the level set must be applied identically to both prediction and ground truth, or to neither. Never apply it to only one side — a one-sided redistance becomes the dominant (and only) error source.
- **Separate the two error sources.** Report the t = 0 reconstruction error (GP posterior vs. true initial field) separately from the forecast error at t > 0. Do not let a t = 0 reconstruction artifact masquerade as forecast skill.
- **Circularity / leakage self-check.** Before reporting, confirm and state in the report: (a) predictions come from the GP, not the simulator; (b) no ground-truth field or parameters were used in inference; (c) the GP is non-degenerate. If the error field is ~zero everywhere except a tiny region, or the metrics look implausibly good, treat it as a red flag of a circular evaluation and investigate before reporting.
